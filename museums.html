Museums in Mexico City

Museo Nacional de Antropología

One of the most important museums in Latin America, this space dives deep into Mexico’s pre-Hispanic civilizations—Aztec, Maya, Olmec, and beyond. It houses the famous Sun Stone and spans entire reconstructed temples, tombs, and ceremonial spaces. Easily rivals (or beats) MoMA in terms of cultural weight and immersive design.

📍 Google Maps

Museo Soumaya

A free-entry museum in a futuristic silver building housing art by Rodin, Dalí, Rivera, and European masters. It's part of Carlos Slim’s private collection, with a mix of European and Mexican art.

🌐 Visit Official Website

📍 Google Maps

Museo Jumex

A compact contemporary art museum next to Soumaya. Features rotating exhibitions of big names like Warhol, Koons, and Gabriel Orozco. A great add-on if you’re already in Polanco.

🌐 Visit Official Website

📍 Google Maps

Frida Kahlo Museum (Casa Azul)

Located in the colorful Coyoacán neighborhood, the Frida Kahlo Museum offers a personal look at the artist's life, work, and home. It's filled with original paintings, personal belongings, and vibrant Mexican décor. The house itself is part of the experience, painted a striking blue and surrounded by a garden courtyard.

🌐 Visit Official Website

📍 Google Maps

Alfredo Guati Rojo National Watercolor Museum *

This charming museum, located in the heart of Coyoacán, is dedicated entirely to the art of watercolor. Housed in a colonial-style home with a peaceful garden, it features a rotating collection of Mexican and international artists. A quiet, lesser-known gem—perfect to pair with a visit to the nearby Frida Kahlo Museum.

* Close to the Frida Kahlo Museum — about a 5–10 minute walk.
