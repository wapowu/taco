El Turix

El Turix is a small, no-frills spot tucked into a side street in Polanco. It’s standing-room only, with a steady stream of locals passing through. The place serves just one thing—cochinita pibil—in the form of tacos, tortas, and panuchos. The pork is marinated in sour orange and achiote, slow-cooked until tender, and topped with pickled red onions. Simple setup, focused menu, and fast service.

El Huequito

El Huequito is a narrow, understated spot just south of Polanco, known for its tacos al pastor. The meat is marinated, stacked on a vertical spit, and shaved off as it cooks—usually served with a slice of pineapple. It’s been around since 1959, with a standing counter and a few seats inside. Simple setup, fast service, focused on one thing done well.

Tacos Los Alexis

Tacos Los Alexis is a humble yet acclaimed taquería in Roma Norte, known for its tacos de suadero and long evening lines. They specialize in slow-cooked meats like suadero, longaniza, and cabeza, grilled to perfection and served with house salsas. Open late into the night, it's a go-to spot for locals and visitors chasing an authentic late-night taco fix. Casual setup, quick service, and a cult following.

Taquería El Califa de León

This humble taco stand in San Rafael made history as the first taco stand in the world to earn a Michelin star. Known for its ultra-simple presentation, El Califa de León serves just a few cuts of meat—like tenderloin (*gaonera*), chuleta, costilla, and bistec— grilled and placed on a handmade corn tortilla. No toppings, no garnishes—just salt, lime, and optional house salsa. It's all about letting the meat speak for itself.

La Casa de Los Tacos *

Located just a few blocks from the Frida Kahlo Museum in Coyoacán, this humble spot serves handmade tortillas, cochinita, birria, and other local favorites. Great for a casual, satisfying meal right after your museum visit. No-frills, very local, and beloved by the neighborhood crowd. Seating is limited but turnaround is quick. Walk-ins welcome.

* Close to the Frida Kahlo Museum — perfect for a post-museum lunch.
